History

Al-Haj Noor Mia College, established in 1995 by Mustafa Kamal and situated in Kankapait Chauddagram Comilla, stands as a beacon of educational excellence. Under the leadership of its non-government Principal, Shahnaz Akter Lovely, the institution has consistently upheld its commitment to providing quality education. With a focus on holistic development, the college offers a diverse range of academic programs and boasts well-equipped facilities including classrooms, laboratories, a library, sports amenities, and computer labs. Al-Haj Noor Mia College remains dedicated to shaping the minds of future generations and fostering an environment conducive to learning and growth.

Mustafa Kamal

Founder

Mustafa Kamal was born on February 2, 1955, in Budin village of Kankapait Union, Chauddagram Upazila, Comilla district, Bangladesh. He comes from a respected Muslim family, with his father, Alhaj Nur Mia, and mother, Ayesha Nur, being his guiding figures. Among his siblings, Mustafa Kamal is the eldest. After completing his education, he moved to Dhaka. In 1995, driven by the desire to spread the light of education in the area, he founded Alhaj Nur Mia College with a focus on affordability. Alongside his devout and compassionate wife, Beauty Akhtar, Mustafa Kamal receives unwavering support and cooperation in his endeavors.

Principle

The principal's vision for Al-Haj Noor Mia College is to create a vibrant learning atmosphere that inspires students to succeed both academically and personally. Our goal is to foster critical thinking, ethics, and leadership skills. Through innovative teaching and support, we aim to empower students to thrive and make meaningful contributions to society.
